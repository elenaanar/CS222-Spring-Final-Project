# Stage 1: Initial Agent + Workflow Design

Stage 1 evaluates how you think about proposal writing and how you translate that understanding into an initial agent or prototype.

A polished proposal is not required in this stage.

## Due Date

Stage 1 is due Friday, June 5, 2026, 11:59 PM Pacific Time.

Late submissions are accepted until Sunday, June 7, 2026, 11:59 PM Pacific Time with a 20% penalty.

Stage 1 grading is based on the submitted 5-minute presentation video. The in-person presentation is mandatory, but it is not used as a separate grading artifact. Use the in-person presentation to show your motivation, idea, and goal, and to exchange feedback with classmates.

## Required Deliverables

Submit:

- initial agent or prototype demo artifact
- 5-minute presentation video or link
- mandatory in-person presentation for demonstration and feedback
- optional screenshots or interaction trace

## What To Design

Design a workflow for turning a rough research idea into a stronger proposal through agent-student interaction. You do not need to follow the starter design.

At minimum, explain:

- the main workflow stages;
- what the agent does automatically and where the student gives feedback;
- how the workflow improves proposal quality over time;
- what artifact the workflow produces for Stage 2 Part 1 or Stage 2 Part 2.

## Required External Research

Look for proposal-writing resources beyond the provided course guide.

Examples:

- funded proposal, PhD proposal, fellowship, or grant-writing examples;
- guides about novelty, gap, evaluation, or proposal structure;
- AI-agent workflow, critique, or revision patterns.

Mention the most important sources in your video or demo artifact. You do not need to submit a separate sources file for Stage 1.

## Presentation Video And In-Person Feedback

Your 5-minute video should show:

- the initial agent or prototype;
- the workflow design;
- one example user journey;
- what you plan to refine in Stage 2.

The goal is to explain your design, not to show a finished proposal.

The in-person presentation is required, but it is not separately graded. Use it to explain your motivation, idea, and goal; ask for suggestions; compare workflow designs; and identify what to refine before Stage 2.

## Stage 1 Checklist

- Initial agent or prototype is demonstrated.
- Workflow has clear stages.
- External proposal-writing sources are cited.
- Student-agent interaction is specific enough to understand.
- The design includes some form of evaluation or revision.
- Video explains why this workflow should produce better proposals.
